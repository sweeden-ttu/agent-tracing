Origin of this paper

This paper began as an email.  On 17 February 2026 the first
author wrote to Dr. Zhang and his colleagues asking whether a
small arithmetic grammar - a few lines of EBNF describing
expressions, terms, identifiers, and whitespace - could be
used to bound the behaviour of an autonomous agent.
The grammar itself is unimportant; what mattered was the
question.  Dr. Zhang's reply opened the line of inquiry that,
by way of the start language  and its recursive enumeration
into, became the producer-consumer construction of this
paper.  The question on 17 February was not about any single Kaggle
competition; it was about whether the Chomsky hierarchy could be
made to do real work in agent design.  This paper is the
considered answer to that email.

The path between the email and the manuscript was not the path
the first author had planned.  The semester involved a missed
midterm in Dr. Namin's software verification and validation
course - a setback that, in any other semester, would have
been recoverable through the usual academic channels.  It also
involved a hospitalisation with a head injury during the
final examination of Dr. Zhang's automata course.  Both
courses had to be passed; the conventional routes were closed.
Dr. Namin's response was characteristic of his teaching: he
extended the offer that publication of substantive original
work in the VV tradition would be accepted in lieu of the
midterm and that the course grade would reflect that.
Dr. Zhang continued to engage substantively with the
producer-consumer construction long after the automata final
had passed, treating the unfinished work as a continuation of
the course rather than a casualty of it.

Both offers were acts of professional grace.  This paper is
their occasion.  The first author owes the publication - and
the path through both courses - to those two decisions, and
to the students and colleagues who carried administrative and
intellectual load during the recovery.  The work is real;
the constraint that produced it was real; the mentorship that
made the constraint navigable was real.  We document the
sequence not for sympathy but for accuracy.  Papers are
sometimes written in a straight line and sometimes in a
crooked one, and it is not dishonourable to say which.

Recognising Julia B. Robinson

A note is owed on the most consequential non-laureate citation
of this paper.  Julia Bowman Robinson (1919-1985) is the
mathematician whose work, with Martin Davis, Hilary Putnam, and
Yuri Matiyasevich, established that the recursively enumerable
subsets of  are exactly the Diophantine subsets
- the modern characterisation of the recursively enumerable
class and the resolution of Hilbert's tenth
problem robinson1949definability,davis1961decision.  The
central undecidability claim that motivates our two-tier
Type-0/Type-3 architecture is, in its modern form, her
result.

Robinson did not receive the ACM A.M. Turing Award.  We are
under no illusion as to why.  The Award was first conferred in
1966 and through 2025 has been given overwhelmingly to men -
a pattern that the field's own histories now acknowledge as a
reflection of decades of structural bias in who was admitted to
graduate study, hired into faculty positions, nominated for
prizes, and otherwise allowed to participate in the
institutional life of computer science and mathematics.
Several of Robinson's contemporaries did receive the Award; she
did not.  Her work, however, is foundational to this paper, and
we cite it as the foundational contribution it is.  Robinson is
listed in the bibliography alongside Turing, Chomsky, and Aho
not as an exception but because she belongs there on the
mathematics.

Robinson was the first woman elected to the National Academy of
Sciences in mathematics (1976), the first woman President of the
American Mathematical Society (1983), and a MacArthur Fellow
(1983).  We name her contributions in the form they took: the
Robinson hypothesis, the
Davis-Putnam-Robinson theorem, the resolution of
Hilbert's tenth.  Where the field's awards have been silent, the
mathematics is not.

Reproducibility

The start language at
examples/rogii/traces/preprocessing/baseline_column_transformer/trace_language.csv,
the recursively enumerated traces across six variants under
examples/rogii/traces/preprocessing/{variant}/, the LaTeX/PDF source
of this paper, the bibliography refs.bib, and the Kaggle notebooks
that will produce the Rogii submission are released at

https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/rogii-wellbore-geology-prediction.

Re-runs at fixed random seeds reproduce the binding's class
signature; placement on the public leaderboard varies by
10-20 ranks across non-deterministic LLM samples, as
discussed in Section sec:discussion.

Funding

The empirical study was conducted on the first author's personal
laptop, with overflow runs on the Texas Tech HPCC.  No external
grant funding was used.  The choice was deliberate as well as
practical: the paper's claim is that the architecture is
operationally competitive within the resource envelope of a
single commodity machine augmented by university HPC, and the
experimental setup honours that claim.
