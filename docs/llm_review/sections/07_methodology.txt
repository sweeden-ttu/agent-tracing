Methodology: The Goal-Driven/Data-Driven Architecture

This section describes the architecture in operational terms.
The architecture is austere by design: one Goal-Driven Type-0
generator, one Data-Driven Type-3 verifier, and an explicit
recursive enumeration procedure that maps the start language to
the realised trace language.  The Type-2 and Type-1 sub-agents
appear as derivations of the binding, not as independent design
choices.

Hoare-triple obligation per step

Each step of Algorithm alg:enum carries a Hoare-triple
obligation in the sense of
Hoare hoare1969axiomatic and
Dijkstra dijkstra1975guarded:

P_tw_tP_t+1

where  is the precondition holding on the state space
before the -th emitted operation, and  is the
postcondition that the verifier checks via its
 transition.  When the precondition fails,
the verifier rejects; when the postcondition fails, the verifier
also rejects; the trace is admitted iff every Hoare triple
along the emitted path holds.

This is the formal sense in which the architecture is
verification-by-construction: the verifier's DFA is the
mechanical realisation of a finite Hoare-style proof obligation
for the Goal-Driven generator's emitted trace.
