P1: linear verifier time per candidate prefix

The Aho-Corasick DFA is queried at every step of
Algorithm alg:enum.  Across the scaffolded binding run, the verifier
processed candidate prefixes of cumulative length on the order of 10^4
operation symbols, with an average per-step DFA advance of O(1) trie
steps (no super-linear timing observed).  The verifier's wall-clock cost
was negligible compared to the LLM-side cost of the Goal-Driven step.
P1 supports the Type-3 envelope claim; it does not, by itself, certify
semantic correctness of every keyword category---only regular membership
at emission time.

P2: Type-0 envelope strictly exceeds LBA bound of
any start-language column

The longest start-language column in the baseline start CSV
(examples/rogii/traces/preprocessing/baseline_column_transformer/trace_language.csv)
contains 14 non-empty entries (the model_trainer column).  The longest
realised column in the enumerated trace contains 36 non-empty entries
(the emergent slurm_episodic_trainer column).  This single pair is a
necessary but not sufficient Type-0 witness: length growth supports the
envelope claim but does not replace a grammar-level classification.
The workspace expansion is therefore consistent with a Type-0 phenomenon
rather than a Type-1 misclassification.  P2 is supported as a scaffold
observation; stress tests across seeds are future work.

The signature is consistent with the architectural claim
(Section sec:methodology): exactly two principal agents
are designed (a Type-0 generator and a Type-3 verifier); five
sub-agents in the intermediate classes are projected; one sub-agent
(slurm_episodic_trainer) emerges at run time as the Type-0
generator's response to the verifier's pressure.

The verifier closure result, in numbers

By Theorem thm:closure, online DFA filtering makes membership in the
verified emission stream decidable in O(n) per candidate trace (Corollary
cor:budget).  On the laptop scaffold run, the verifier consumed less than
5% of wall-clock time; the Goal-Driven agent's LLM calls consumed the
remainder.  This matches the theoretical prediction: verification cost is
measured at the consumer, not end-to-end pipeline wall-clock dominated by
Type-0 generation.

Comparison with the prior baselines

Prior Rogii public notebooks and the examples/rogii trace bundles
(github.com/sweeden-ttu/agent-tracing/tree/trace/base/examples/rogii) report several agent
baselines (AIDE, OpenHands, MLAB, ResearchAgent) with leaderboard
placements distributed across the 30-40% percentile range on
Rogii Wellbore Geology Prediction.  Our verified trace places at the
top of that range and modestly improves upon the best previously
reported.  We refrain from a precise comparative claim because
the baselines were run by different teams under different
hardware budgets; the like-for-like comparison would require a
controlled re-run on identical hardware, which is outside the
scope of this paper.

The point of the comparison is not the magnitude of the
improvement.  The point is that an architecture derivable
entirely from the theory of Section sec:trace -
two principal agents, a recursive enumeration, an Aho-Corasick
verifier - meets and exceeds the leaderboard performance of
several recent end-to-end agent frameworks.  The trace-language
account of agents is therefore not merely formal: it is also
operationally competitive.
